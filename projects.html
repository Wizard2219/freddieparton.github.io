Featured Projects

This page focuses on the projects and technical build work behind my sailing career. Alongside my degree research, I am building tools for yacht racing data analysis and performance review, and I will keep adding more detail here as the work develops.

Wind Shear Modelling

My MSc work focused on wind structure and boundary-layer meteorology, with a particular interest in how vertical wind variation affects performance and forecasting quality. It sharpened my understanding of the fine-scale weather details that matter in racing and routing.

Focus: boundary-layer meteorology, wind structure, forecast interpretation.

Oceanography Research

My oceanography degree projects focused on physical oceanography and climate variability, including work on deep-water currents in the western Mediterranean Basin and research into links between El Nino events and the North Atlantic Oscillation.

Focus: physical oceanography, basin-scale circulation, climate signals.

Yacht Racing Data Analysis App

I am building an app for yacht racing data analysis to make it easier to review performance, compare sessions, and turn raw onboard data into something useful for sailors and navigators. The goal is to bring together tracks, wind, boat performance, and event context in one workflow that is fast enough to use after sailing and detailed enough to support real decisions.

Focus: performance review, visualisation, workflow design, racing insight.

Routing & Forecast Workflow Development

Alongside formal projects, I spend a lot of time refining my own workflow for routing, forecast interpretation, and technical race preparation. That includes combining model data, currents, observations, and performance thinking into tools and processes that are practical under time pressure.

Focus: routing logic, weather workflows, race preparation tools.
