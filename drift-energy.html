Drift Energy

Overview

Drift Energy is building a new class of ocean-going renewable infrastructure: autonomous high-performance sailing vessels that harvest wind energy at sea and deliver it globally. Rather than fixing turbines to land or seabed, Drift's ships chase optimal weather conditions across the open ocean, generating more power than a static wind turbine and delivering it directly to ports anywhere on earth.

The company calls it net positive shipping — maritime infrastructure that produces more energy than it consumes. Featured in The Economist, CNN, and The Sunday Times, and backed by leading DeepTech and Ocean funds, Drift has drawn serious attention from the energy and maritime industries.

How It Works

Generation

  • High-performance sailing vessels cruise the world’s oceans following from our bespoke routing software to stay in optimal weather conditions, harvesting energy from underwater turbines as they sail.
  • By following weather patterns rather than sitting in one location, vessels can achieve load factors up to 7× those of conventional renewable sources.
  • Using apparent wind for energy generation enhances the efficiency of the ships and results in higher wind power density than conventional offshore wind turbines 200 m above sea level.

Storage & Distribution

  • Each vessel is a self-contained renewable power plant: generation of energy from the turbines, conversion into hydrogen with the electrolyser and storage in onboard tanks.
  • Energy is delivered directly into ports in containerized solutions, bypassing the need for transmission infrastructure, land leasing, or lengthy permitting.
  • Target markets include port bunkering, hard-to-abate heavy industry, island nations accessing the green economy, and maritime shipping decarbonisation.

Scale & Impact

  • Oceans cover 70% of the planet — Drift’s technology unlocks an energy landscape vastly larger than any land-based source.
  • The mission at scale: a flotilla on every ocean, producing green hydrogen for all applications. The raw materials for a single Cape class ship could build over 300 Drift ships, equivalent to 2000 GWh/year of renewable energy.
  • Easy to build and fast to deploy, this assembly of existing technologies can be scaled rapidly to meet global energy demands and the first of its kind will be capable of producing energy at a competitive price - a first for any class of renewable.
