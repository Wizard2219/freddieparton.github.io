Racing Experience

Overview

My racing background combines years of high-level sailing with a technical understanding of the environment the boats are racing in. I started in dinghies, notably as a top youth Laser sailor in France, and have since developed into big-boat inshore and offshore programs where navigation, weather interpretation, and performance analysis are central.

Key Campaigns

Dinghies — Laser / ILCA

  • Raced at the top of youth Laser sailing in France, competing at national level from a young age.
  • French Youth National Laser Champion.
  • Strong foundations in boatspeed, shifts, and race management that underpin everything since.

Admiral’s Cup

  • Competed in the Admiral’s Cup, one of the most prestigious offshore team racing events in the world.
  • Offshore racing at the highest IRC level, combining inshore stadium racing with demanding offshore legs.

Maxi Yachts — Jethou & Highland Fling

  • Sailed on Jethou, a highly competitive IRC 52 campaigned hard across the UK and European offshore circuit.
  • Programme includes the RORC offshore season, Round the Island Race, Fastnet, and major inshore regattas.
  • Sailed on Highland Fling, a series of powerful offshore maxis with a strong track record in long-distance racing.

IRC Offshore — Bulldog & others

  • Raced on Bulldog across the IRC offshore circuit, competing in class against strong UK and European programs.
  • Broad IRC campaign experience across boats including Amplifi, Merlin, and Comet, covering inshore and offshore racing.
  • Increasing focus on navigation and technical preparation as programs developed.

Weather Support — TP52, RC44, Maxi & J-Class

  • Provided race-specific forecast and routing support for teams across the TP52, RC44, Maxi, J-Class, and Olympic circuits through MetWind.
  • Working with top programs across the European and world sailing calendar, from Palma to Porto Cervo.

Roles

I bring value where sailing judgment and technical preparation meet — navigation, weather strategy, offshore routing, race preparation, performance analysis, and helping teams turn complex information into clear action on the water.

The modern navigator’s role is where I am most at home: integrating weather, ocean state, boat performance, and instruments into decisions that drive both tactical execution and long-term campaign learning.
